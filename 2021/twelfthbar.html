SNAPSHOT

A dynamic branding system concept for a fictional jazz club and restaurant, The 12th Bar.

OVERVIEW

The driving idea behind this branding project was to combine the style of 1950s and 60s jazz album cover design with modern 21st century design standards and trends. This combination of old and new was intended to match the intent, style and direction of the restaurant itself.

The bold serif typography and strong contrasting colors used in this project were inspired by a series of iconic jazz and blues record covers designed by Reid Miles for famous jazz label, Blue Note Records. These designs were dynamic, avant-garde, unpredictable, and attention-grabbing, all characteristics that represent jazz music.

The dynamic and variable logo marks were developed as a way to represent the chaotic yet organized nature of jazz, as well as its improvisational and unpredictable nature. Using a series of marks that had similar but non-identical elements and structures allowed for layouts and designs that fit within a system, yet could be unique, unpredictable and improvisational at heart.
